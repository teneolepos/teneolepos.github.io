Reinvorgating a classic brand to support your health

www.harvardpilgrim.org/hapiguide/

Strategy / Design / Product Management

Harvard Pilgrim Health Care (HPHC) felt like they were losing touch with their customer base and launched a new campaign to raise awareness about the brand.

Specifically, the client wanted to engage and connect with individuals, employers and brokers through new communication.

Impact

  • Saw 3% uptick in engagement
  • Reinvigorated brand to be more relevant
  • Evolved initial campaign to be a dedicated content hub and newsletter

Design

Health insurance is one of the most complex topics. Our content strategy focused on creating information for our different users to help navigate the system as well as better understand HPHC's plans. I was given the content strategy and asked to think about the best way to incorporate the new, fun campaign within the more tactical company website. We also had to meet a short development timeline while facing challenges of legacy platforms and different vendors.

The main KPI was user engagement as determined by the number of email sign ups and whitepaper downloads. Since both these actions were not dependent on the home website, I determined that a microsite would be suitable for the new campaign content and allow flexibility for development on a separate platform.

In order to motivate users to perform these two actions, I kept the sitemap small. Key content pieces were placed at the top of pages to encourage people to see value in signing up and seek access to additional gated content.

To meet quick turnaround times, I designed in an atomic way that could be quickly built in a CMS.

Product Management

I also took on the responsibilities of product manager and content editor in the CMS. I defined the business rules for how form submission and article access would function for the developers. I collaborated with the creative team to discuss which atomic blocks were appropriate for content and built out the pages in the CMS. Finally, I managed conversations between the different stakeholders and our developer and QA teams to implement changes and successfully launch the site.

Product Evolution

We saw record enagement on the campaign site. As a result, HPHC felt confident to have us evolve the idea. Knowing the great need to improve health insurance literacy, we proposed a permanent content space on the home site paired with a newsletter. For the strategy, I designed an experience that continued to segment our audience by customer type and created categories based on customer content goals around information on wellness, insurance education and healthcare news. We named the content hub "The HaPi Guide".

I worked with the UI designer to develop the digital brand and styleguide. I then served as product manager to develop the site. I gave guidance to developers to build and style components while working within the CMS to then create pages and implement content. I further reviewed the site and worked with QA to ensure a successful launch.
