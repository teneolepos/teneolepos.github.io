Promoting patient connections for a DTC drug

www.cosentyx.com

Content Strategy / UX / CMS

Novartis Pharmaceuticals was seeking to redesign the website to align with their campaign that featured real patients. They also hoped to use the channel as a way to provide better education, patient support and promote patient adherence on drug.

Impact

  • Helped clients commit to a user-centric mindset towards product design
  • Implemented and embedded an atomic design system for all brands and future projects at Novartis
  • Led the client to conduct user research for the first time
  • Developed a personalized experience based on condition

Product Strategy

The brand team lacked strategic direction when it came to managing their digital assets. Product requests and updates were based on business or personal perception - not by user needs.

I saw this as a big gap. To help them develop a sustainable model for digital product managment, I took them through a patient-centric design process

Design Process

  • Conduct user research
  • Develop personas
  • Create patient journeys
  • Define vision statement
  • Identify design principles

“To be the digital destination that our audience is driven to seek out to find further hope for regaining themselves from PsO, PsA and AS through a real, honest experience that demonstrates the complete (Accessibility, Treatment and Advocacy) experience COSENTYX® provides them.”

New Client Vision Statement

Information Architecture

Before starting wireframes, I conducted an audit of existing content on the site. I also looked across the digital landscape to see what patients had questions or concerns around that were not being addressed by traditional resources.

Based on a better understanding of user needs and their information seeking behavior, I developed a new sitemap with basic levels of personalization. This became a website experience that was indication specific and allowed for navigation to key content that we knew were important for patients.

Design

With an understanding of the content and assets, I introduced the concept of atomic design to the client.

This was especially relevant at a critical point when Novartis was shifting to a new CMS platform.

Together with the UI designer, we developed a new digital brand identity and design system for the client.

Product Management

I worked closely with the digital producer to manage the third party vendor in developing the new CMS and implementing our design.

    This included:
  • defining business requirements
  • writing and reviewing Jira stories
  • serving as a content editor in the CMS
  • QAing and providing feedback
