How Apple may improve electronic health systems (EHS)

Design / Case Study

Adobe XD / Axure

iOS Tablet

Developed concept to envision how Apple may improve our healthcare system. Conducted end-to-end design process to produce a high-fidelity prototype of Tami (Translational Artificial Medical Intelligence), cousin of Siri. Tami addresses physician burnout and administrative inefficiencies by translating human handwriting into activities within an EHS.

Inspired by Apple’s presentation style at WWDC, I developed the storyline for Tami as if I was introducing her to the world for the first time.

Side Project

Studies show that the use of notifications and checklists have led to a decrease in treatment errors. I concepted a view for Apple Watch of what a notification may look like from an Apple EHS. I chose the Apple Watch because it is the best device to present physicians with the most timely notifications. The prototype was designed in Adobe XD.

Empathize

In order to come up with a new product for Apple, I started by assessing their healthcare technologies. Their recent foray into Health Records gave me the inspiration to focus on this area. My research also showed that Apple was already trying to address the largest problem in EHS — interoperability — through their health kit suite.

I also looked into some of Apple’s other healthcare ventures, including their doctors office for employees called AC Wellness. After finding the patient login portal, I noticed that the company was using athenahealth as their EHR. The design of Health Records mimicked the key framework of athenahealth.

With confidence that something involving an EHS was a logical product decision for Apple, I identified physician burnout from using these systems as the key challenge I wanted to solve.

User Research

I reached out to 5 clinicians from varying experience levels: 2 practicing physicians, 1 who just completed residency and 2 medical school students. I also reached out to an Epic employee to empathize with the physician journey from different perspectives.

From their stories, I developed a persona and the physician journey using an EHS.

Define

After all this research I felt stuck because it seemed like Apple and athenahealth were already addressing many of these challenges. athenahealth prides itself on having conducted countless hours of user research to get to their final design, and it has paid off.

I began re-pouring over my interview transcripts to see if I could dig out a nugget of inspiration. The story a physician told about his amazing medical transcriptionist Tammy, caught my eye. Tammy was “so good” because she understood each physicians’ individual scribbles. She also knew when physicians left things out in their notes or scripts and could fill it in on their behalf.

Her story, and the coincidental rhyming of her name, gave birth to Tami, Translational Artificial Medical Intelligence.
