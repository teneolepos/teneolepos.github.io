Helping scientists be better scientists

Design / Case Study

Developed concept for a user portal for the Human Cell Atlas (HCA), a project in the Science division at Chan Zuckerberg Initiative (CZI). Addressed pain points in the data upload journey by bioinformaticians and computational biologists. Created a new, innovative micro-funding program.

Empathize & Define

I began my research by trying to understand the world of single-cell science and how data amongst labs are being accessed. I did basic research of various transcription technologies and the existing data platforms. I noted that HCA lacked a user portal for scientists to upload their work — the current process was all email based.

Next, I reached out to over 40 scientists who had contributed their data to conduct user research. I also spoke to designers of other large data platforms to understand their way of approaching the problem.

With this research I developed a persona and user journey to map out scientists' pain points, specifically in the data upload phase.

Design

I used Figma to build out the prototype in desktop view. Most scientists hold their files locally and the complexity of information requires more viewing area.

The layout of the portal mimics similar interaction patterns of similar platforms that users will be exposed to. The main navigation follows the key steps of the user journey. The data upload process falls under the “Contribute” phase.

New Features

  • Cloud upload option
  • Progress tracker
  • Server status pane

Innovation

The mission of the Science initiative at CZI is “to help scientists be better scientists”. I used this to think empathetically about what drives scientists to do what they do. My hypothesis is that the simple dream to make an impact, is the core driver for many who have chosen this path.

Two large pain points in the science community are the constant search for funding and limited collaboration. In order to address these problems, I developed a concept called “Nucleo Funding”. It is a gamified way to motivate scientists to upload and contribute their work, and get small amounts of supportive funding as a result (hence “nucleo”). It further phrases the opportunity to be impact focused — the more initiatives a scientist contributes to, the greater the impact and the greater the amount of micro-funding.

I designed a section of the journey/navigation called Impact. Scientists have a dashboard view to see their contributions and funding to date. They can also seek new opportunities to contribute to a category of initiatives and follow what the rest of the community is doing as well. The layout for the social view follows the look and feel of a similar product from CZI called Meta.
