Engaging girls to play soccer

Product Strategy / Design

A national UK sports organization wanted to understand how they could break traditional barriers and societal stereotypes to encourage young girls to play soccer.

I helped conduct user research and market analysis that led to a product strategy to motivate girls to play soccer. I then brought the idea to life through a hi-fidelity app prototype.

User Research

We had a short time frame, so I worked with another team member to recruit family and friends as participants for user research. We wanted to understand:

  • their perception of soccer
  • their experience with soccer (playing, watching, etc.)
  • current barriers to participation and engagement with soccer
  • things they thought would increase their participation and engagement with soccer

The user research helped form the foundation of the two personas. “Tasha” represented the target audience of disengaged youth and the secondary persona represented the more engaged, but smaller audience segment. To make the personas more robust, I weaved in market research on digital trends that resonated with the target audience.

Key concepts that stood out from user research: influencer social engagement through various channels and media forms, AR (PokemonGo was really hot at the time), safety and security both on the digital platform and in the physical world (since the girls are all under legal age).

Product Strategy

In order to deliver on the key user goals, I came up with a product strategy that would be delivered through an app. I worked with my team to come up with two journeys around this concept.

The first journey was what we hypothesized to be a more realistic scenario that involved Tasha going to a school soccer event. Through making new friends and engaging with them in the app, she begins to become more involved with soccer.

The second journey was a more “stretch” journey that involved Tasha going to an event sponsored by the famous Youtube blogger Zoella. There she makes friends and downloads the app. By participating in the online community, playing the interactive AR soccer game, and tracking her soccer performance in the app, Tasha becomes an active soccer player.

In the followup testing, users connected with the “stretch” story. They believed that an influencer would really motivate them to go to a soccer event, launching that initial touchpoint. The chat and community features also resonated with how they connect with their friends today. Furthermore, the AR soccer game with gamification also excited the girls.

Design

I built wireframes and a hi-fidelity prototype of the app to align with our “stretch” journey. I used Axure which allowed me to create more advanced microinteractions and mimic animations like swiping to shoot a ball. Based off user research, the four main tabs of the app were Home, Events, Challenge, and Chat. I also designed the user profile to show how technology platforms can support personal growth.

For visual design, I took inspiration from the client’s brand, a past campaign they did to engage girls, existing sports apps and current design trends.

To refine the features, I looked at “best-in-class” apps across industries. For the user profile, I referenced fitness and sports gaming apps to gain insight on a good reporting experience for showcasing stats and presenting scoreboards. I also looked to ticketmaster, instagram, UXBear, and many others for inspiration on event registration, social media sharing, and chat integration.

My research for the user profile also led me to the adidas miCoach Smart Ball. The technology of IoT and its ability to capture a number of important stats around playing performance motivated me to incorporate it within the prototype as a way of helping Tasha see improvement over time as she engaged more with soccer.
