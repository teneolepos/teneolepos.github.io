Improving patient education and adherence for a multi-indication drug

www.xarelto-us.com

Strategy / UX

Janssen Pharmaceuticals was seeking to redesign the website of their top cardiovascular drug after being approved for a number of new indications. They needed to account for new patient populations that will now be visiting the site. The client also wanted to improve the site to provide better education, patient support and promote adherence on drug.

Impact

  • Launched an 100+ page website with a personalized journey based on condition
  • Helped clients commit to a user-centric mindset towards product design
  • Led client to conduct user research for the first time
  • Implemented and embedded an atomic design system

Product Strategy

I conducted an audit of the site and realized that we were not talking to patients in the most effective way. In addition, the client needed content for patients with these new conditions that will now be coming on site.

With our content strategist, I developed personas and user journeys to help identify pieces of content to eliminate, revise and create for the new site.

From this work, I created a new sitemap that better addressed the information seeking needs of patients and allowed for a more personalized, indication specific journey on the website.

User Testing

The client had never done user testing before. I convinced them that given the complexity of managing different patient needs and the scale of the site, it would be worth the effort.

I managed a third party vendor to conduct research and developed the prototype for testing.

User Testing Goals

  1. Determine the best way to start patients off on the correct indication journey
  2. Confirm unique interaction patterns
  3. Get feedback on new content ideas

Design

Aside from usability concerns, the old website lacked consistency in design. Furthermore, development time was long to accomodate unique requests.

To address these challenges, I introduced the concept of atomic design to the client. I worked together with the UI designer to create a visual design system.

The client further requested templates that could be reused in the future. I designed around 50 new templates using the atomic blocks that fulfilled content needs while still making it feasible for implementation in the CMS platform.
