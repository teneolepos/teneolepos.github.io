Educating patients about Ankylosing Spondylitis

www.monsterpainintheAS.com

Content Strategy / Design / Product Management

Ankylosing Spondylitis (AS) is a hard to diagnose condition and many people confuse it with inflammatory back pain (IBP).

Novartis Pharmaceuticals wanted to launch an unbranded campaign to help educate around this condition. Specifically, they hoped to use a quiz to provide guidance and motivate patients to speak with their doctor.

Impact

  • 127,000+ quiz completions since launch
  • Work mentioned in Parade, People, MSNBC, Fox News, etc.

Product Strategy

In addition to research already obtained by the brand, I searched the digital landscape to better understand what conversations patients were having around this topic and the resources they were using.

I used all these materials to inform the sitemap. The content journey flows linearly after someone gets their results from the quiz to educate them around IBP and AS.

Design

Because the campaign is unbranded, I had a lot more freedom in design. The assets centered around a single campaign video. I worked with the UI designer and developers to create a more interactive experience using cutdowns from the video to integrate animation and movement in the site without distracting users from the core educational content.

Quiz and Doctor Finder

The quiz was originally developed by a third party vendor. There was a set criteria on what qualified someone to have AS as well as multiple exit points for those who may only have IBP. To pull the quiz onto our site, I created logic trees and user flows to guide developers.

We also wanted to help patients find a rheumatologist by integrating a doctor finder tool from a third party vendor on our platform. This gave us design flexibility without having to drive to a separate platform. I used empirical research and behavioral insight to identify the most important fields to drive physician choice.

Product Management

I worked closely with the development team to define the business rules for the quiz and the doctor finder tool.

I then collaborated with the UI designer and the development team to integrate and tweak animations to deliver an engaging experience to better understand AS.
